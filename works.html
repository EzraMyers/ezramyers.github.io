Works shown publicly for Laws of Folk, with selected pieces held back for the exhibition.

Pay the Devil

202.4 × 152.4 cmInk, charcoal, collage and gold leaf on Bockingford watercolour paper2026

Pay the Devil stands as one of the central works in Laws of Folk. The figure carries masquerade, resistance and inherited force through the language of Jab Jab.

They called us devils. So we became them.

The work does not treat the mask as costume. It treats the mask as knowledge. The figure arrives through the history of Trinidadian Carnival and the memory of bodies forced to turn insult into power.

Mary in the Light

255.8 × 152.4 cmInk, charcoal and collage on Bockingford watercolour paper2019

The figure is held inside light and density, somewhere between portrait and architecture.

This is not a portrait of Mary Seacole. It is a portrait of what she carried.

She came to serve. She was refused. She came anyway. The work holds her as strength, complexity and historical pressure.

Jay

56 × 38 cmInk and charcoal on paper

Jay is shown as an origin point. Smaller in scale, direct in its address, it marks an early moment in the visual language that the larger works would later deepen.

This is where the language began.

Jay is an earlier work. A study. The piece in which the practice found its footing and began to understand what it was trying to say. It is here because the full body of work would be dishonest without it.
